AquaPack Robotics at RoboSub 2023

Posted on 11 October 2023

At the end of July, 2023, AquaPack Robotics represented North Carolina State University at RoboSub 2023, held at NIWC TRANSDEC in San Diego, California, USA. Across eight days, the team worked on SeaWolf VIII, undertook five days of competition testing, and completed two semi-finals runs. With a year of hard work and countless hours spent preparing for this competition, the team is proud to announce we placed fourteenth overall at RoboSub 2023.

Scoring for the competition is broken up into multiple parts, with design documentation, the team introductory video, the team's website, a design presentation, and the best competition run all being scored. Our scoring broke down as follows:

Category Placement Points
Technical Design Report 6th 146.1
Technical Presentation 24th 142.56
Website 24th 150.8
Introductory Video 10th 101.27

The team would especially like to thank the Isabella Mormando and the NC State Electrical and Computer Engineering Department for their fantastic help in filiming the team's introductory video for this year.

The points-scoring competition runs are split across a maximum of four phases. Each team is given the opportunity to qualify, two chances at a good semi-finals run, a "third-chance" run for teams who competed in semi-finals but did not make finals, and a finals run. Qualification requires that a team must demonstrate autonomy by successfully navigating through a "gate" without being controlled by the team from the surface. In semi-finals, teams are given the opportunity to complete various tasks, such as firing torpedoes at a target, dropping markers in a box, and identifying and touching a buoy. Each successfully completed task rewards a certain number of points, with the highest scoring teams then moving on to compete in the finals. As previously mentioned, the "third chance" runs allow for any team that qualified for semi-finals, but didn't meet the points threshold for finals, to perform additional points-scoring runs on finals day, with the aim of scoring enough points to meet the threshold for a finals run.

AquaPack completed runs in both alloted semi-finals slots, and attempted a third chance run. [[During the second semi-final run, the team achieved our highest scoring, with a score of 1326 points.]] After semi-finals had completed, the team was placed 13th in competition run scoring, before being bumped to 14th place after an impressive effort by Amador Valley High School, who scored well in their third chance run following two days of robot repair during semi-finals. Some of these repairs required new Blue Robotics Electronics Speed Controllers, which AquaPack gladly supplied to the team, ultimately enabling them to finish fifth overall in competition runs.

The second semi-finals day saw the team's highest points scoring autonomous run, surfacing with 1326 points. 15 additional points were gained as a weight bonus, as SeaWolf VIII's total weight measured in at 34.6 kilograms before the run.
